## TLDR

**You are on solid legal ground.** Congressional voting records are PUBLIC DATA under Peru's Transparency Law (Ley 27806). Showing factual voting records with proper source attribution is protected. The main risks are: (1) electoral propaganda restrictions during the 24h pre-election silence period, and (2) defamation claims if you add subjective commentary beyond facts. Use the model disclaimer provided below and stick to neutral, factual presentation.

## 1. DEFAMATION LAW ANALYSIS

### Legal Framework

| Law | Articles | Description |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Codigo Penal | Art. 130 | Injuria (direct insult) |
| Codigo Penal | Art. 131 | Calumnia (false criminal accusation) |
| Codigo Penal | Art. 132 | Difamacion (defamation via publication) |

### Article 132 - Difamacion (Key Article)

Difamacion requires:
1. **Publication/Communication** to third parties
2. **Harm to honor or reputation**
3. **Intent** (animus difamandi)

Penalties: Up to 2 years imprisonment, increased to 3 years if media is used.

### AMPAY Risk Assessment: LOW

**Why you're protected:**

1. **Exceptio Veritatis (Truth Defense):** Peru recognizes the truth defense. If what you publish is TRUE and verifiable, it cannot constitute defamation. Congressional voting records are official government data.

2. **Animus Criticandi vs Animus Difamandi:** Courts distinguish between intent to criticize (protected) vs intent to defame (punishable). Presenting factual voting data without subjective insults = animus criticandi.

3. **Public Figure Doctrine:** Politicians have reduced privacy protections for their public acts. The Constitutional Tribunal has ruled that public figures must tolerate greater scrutiny. Case law: Jefferson Farfan vs Magaly Medina (Exp. 7387-2019-0) established satire limits, but factual reporting was distinguished.

4. **Actual Malice Standard:** For public figures, Peru applies a higher bar - plaintiff must show publisher knew information was false or showed reckless disregard for truth.

### Key Case Law

- **Exp. 443-2018-0:** Sharing criticism on Facebook found to lack animus difamandi
- **Exp. 2009-01730-0:** Correcting journalistic errors evidences lack of intent to defame
- **Gonzales and Bazan cases:** Courts balanced honor against free expression, favoring public interest

### WARNINGS

- **Ojo Publico precedent:** In 2019, a Peruvian court ordered assets frozen for Ojo Publico journalists in a defamation case. Even frivolous lawsuits can cause operational damage.
- **Strategic lawsuits:** Politicians use defamation suits to intimidate (SLAPP suits). Miguel Arevalo Ramirez filed 10+ lawsuits seeking $500M+ against multiple media outlets.

### Mitigation

- **Stick to facts.** Never editorialize.
- **Document sources.** Every claim must trace to official Congress data.
- **Insurance.** Consider media liability insurance.
- **Legal fund.** Budget for defense against potential SLAPP suits.

## 2. ELECTORAL LAW RESTRICTIONS

### Legal Framework

| Law | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| Ley 26859 | Ley Organica de Elecciones (Art. 181+) |
| Ley 28094 | Ley de Organizaciones Politicas |
| Res. 0112-2025-JNE | Reglamento sobre propaganda electoral (March 2025) |
| Res. 0047-2024-JNE | Previous propaganda regulation (Feb 2024) |

### Key Restrictions

**Silencio Electoral (Electoral Silence):**
- **24 hours before elections:** ALL electoral propaganda prohibited
- **2 days before:** Public political meetings/demonstrations prohibited

**Definition of "Propaganda Electoral":**
JNE defines it as content that **persuades citizens to vote for a specific candidate**. Key elements:
- Persuasive intent
- Call to action
- Promotion of specific candidate/party

### AMPAY Risk Assessment: MEDIUM-LOW

**Why AMPAY is likely NOT propaganda:**

1. **Neutral presentation:** A database showing factual voting records without recommending who to vote for = informational, not propaganda
2. **No persuasive intent:** Presenting "Congressman X voted Y times for Z" is factual
3. **No call to action:** If you don't say "vote for/against," it's not propaganda

**Critical distinction:**
| Content Type | Classification |
|--------------|----------------|
| "Congressman voted against education budget" | FACTUAL - Permitted |
| "Bad congressman! Don't vote for him!" | PROPAGANDA - Prohibited |
| Scoring system ("2/10 transparency score") | GRAY AREA - Could be seen as persuasive |

### Penalties for Violations

- Electoral propaganda violations: 1-5 UIT fine (approx. $1,500-$7,500 USD)
- State publicity/neutrality violations: 30-100 UIT fine (approx. $45,000-$150,000 USD)

### Mitigation

- **During silencio electoral (24h pre-election):** Consider adding prominent banner stating "Informacion con fines educativos. No constituye propaganda electoral."
- **Avoid scoring systems** during electoral periods
- **Remove any persuasive language** from the app entirely

## 3. DATA PROTECTION (LEY 29733)

### Legal Framework

| Regulation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Ley 29733 | Ley de Proteccion de Datos Personales (2011) |
| D.S. 016-2024-JUS | New Regulation (November 2024) |
| ANPDP | Enforcement authority |

### Key Question: Are Congressional Votes "Personal Data"?

**Answer: NO - They are PUBLIC DATA**

### Article 14 Exceptions (No Consent Required)

The law explicitly exempts:

1. **Data for public entity functions** - Congressional votes are part of official government operations
2. **Data in publicly accessible sources** - Congress publishes voting records officially
3. **Data related to credit/solvency** - Not applicable
4. **Regulatory data** - Not applicable

### Are Political Votes "Datos Sensibles"?

Under Art. 2 of Ley 29733, sensitive data includes "political opinions."

**HOWEVER:** This refers to PRIVATE political beliefs, not PUBLIC OFFICIAL ACTS. A congressman's vote in plenary session is:
- An official act of their public function
- Published by Congress itself
- Not a private political opinion

### AMPAY Risk Assessment: VERY LOW

**You are protected because:**

1. **Public source data:** You're republishing what Congress already publishes
2. **Public official exception:** Data about officials' public functions is not protected private data
3. **Public interest:** Transparency in government is a constitutional value (Art. 2.5 of Constitution)
4. **Ley 27806 (Transparency Law):** Explicitly makes government information public

### Enforcement Reality

- ANPDP applied 77 sanctions in 2023 (1.07-100 UIT range)
- Focus is on private sector abuses, not public transparency projects
- No known cases of sanctioning political transparency platforms

## 4. TRANSPARENCY LAW (LEY 27806)

### Your Strongest Protection

**Ley 27806 - Ley de Transparencia y Acceso a la Informacion Publica**

This law:
- Guarantees access to public information (Art. 2.5 of Constitution)
- Requires government entities to publish information proactively
- Makes congressional activities public by default

### Official Data Sources

| Source | URL | Data Available |
|--------|-----|----------------|
| Portal Transparencia Congreso | congreso.gob.pe/transparencia-portal/ | Voting records, attendance |
| Asistencias y Votaciones | congreso.gob.pe/AsistenciasVotacionesPleno/ | Session-by-session votes |
| Mociones Portal | wb2server.congreso.gob.pe/smociones-portal/ | Legislative motions |

### 2024 Updates

- **D.S. 007-2024-JUS** (May 2024): New regulation strengthening transparency
- **Ley 31783:** Modifications to transparency requirements
- **D.L. 1688** (October 2024): Additional provisions

### AMPAY Implications

- **Congressional voting data is PUBLIC by law**
- **You have a constitutional right to access and republish it**
- **Source attribution is good practice but republication is permitted**

## 8. RECOMMENDED LEGAL PROTECTIONS

### Structural

1. **Corporate structure:** Operate through a legal entity (SAC or similar), not as individual
2. **Media liability insurance:** Essential given SLAPP suit risk
3. **Legal defense fund:** Budget 5-10% of operational costs
4. **Coalition membership:** Join press freedom organizations (ANP, CPJ contacts)

### Operational

1. **Source documentation:** Screenshot and archive every data point with timestamp
2. **Editorial policy:** Written policy prohibiting opinion/commentary
3. **Correction mechanism:** Rapid response system for factual errors
4. **Version control:** Track all data updates with audit trail

### Pre-Publication Review

1. **No adjectives:** Remove subjective descriptors
2. **No conclusions:** Present data without interpretation
3. **No recommendations:** Never suggest voting behavior
4. **No scoring:** Avoid "grades" or "rankings" that imply judgment

### Electoral Period Protocol

1. **72h before:** Display electoral period banner
2. **24h before (silencio electoral):** Consider "maintenance mode" or prominent disclaimer
3. **Election day:** Standard operation with prominent informational disclaimer
